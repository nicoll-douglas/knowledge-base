# Physical Layer -  OSI Model Layer 1

## Access Networks

### DSL (Digital Subscriber Line)

- DSL uses existing telephone infrastructure in order to provide internet access to homes
- A DSL modem inside a home takes digital data and translate it into high frequency tones for transmission over telephone wires to the local central office of a telco/ISP
- The telephone line carries both telephone signals and data simultaneously
- The signals are encoded at difference frequences:
    - 0-4kHz band: two-way telephone channel 
    - 4-50kHz band: medium-speed upstream data channel
    - 50kHz-1MHz: high-speed upstream data channel
- On the customer side, a splitter splits data and telephone signals and forwards data signals to the modem

### Cable Internet

- Cable internet access uses television infrastructure in order to provide internet access to homes
- Cable internet uses coaxial cables to provide internet and television signals to homes
- It is a shared broadcast medium meaning every downstream packet travels down every downstream link to every home
- This means that internet may slower for users when traffic is heavy and a lot of bandwidth is taken up

## Physical Media

- Whenever a bit propagates through a network, it will travel across several physical media
- A bit is sent by propagating electromagnetic waves or optical pulses through a physical medium between a transmitter-receiver pair
- Physical media come in two types: guided media and unguided media
- With guided media, the waves are guided along a solid medium (e.g copper cable, optic fibre)
- With unguided media, the waves propagate in the atmosphere or outer space (e.g wireless LAN, digital satellite channel)
